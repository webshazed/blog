Buying Guide: What to Look for in a 60cc Chainsaw

Choosing the right 60cc chainsaw goes beyond just the engine displacement. Consider these critical factors:

  • Engine Type & Technology: A 2-stroke engine is standard for this class. Look for features like X-Torq (Husqvarna) or similar technologies that boost power while lowering fuel use and emissions.
  • Guide Bar Length: 16-18 inches is for light trimming and firewood. 20 inches is the sweet spot for general all-around use. 22-24 inches is for felling and bucking larger trees but adds weight and reduces agility.
  • Power-to-Weight Ratio: A lighter saw with the same power is easier to handle and less fatiguing. Check the dry weight. Premium brands often achieve better ratios through engineering.
  • Anti-Vibration System: This is crucial for comfort and safety. Look for designated systems (e.g., Husqvarna's Low Vibr, Echo's vibration reduction) that isolate the handles from engine and bar vibrations.
  • Chain Brake & Safety: A reliable, easy-to-reach inertia-activated chain brake is non-negotiable. Also consider a robust chain catcher and a good tip guard.
  • Ease of Maintenance: Tool-less air filter covers, easy spark plug access, and a simple chain tensioning system (side or top adjuster) make routine upkeep faster and more likely to be done.
  • Brand Reputation & Support: Established brands (Echo, Husqvarna, Stihl, Poulan Pro) offer better warranty coverage, readily available parts, and a network of service centers. Generic brands are a bigger gamble on long-term support.
