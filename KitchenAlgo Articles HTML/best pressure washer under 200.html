Buying Guide: How to Choose the Best Pressure Washer Under $200

When shopping in this budget range, focus on electric models (gas units typically start well above $200). Here are the key factors to consider:

  • PSI & GPM: For light to medium duty (cars, patio furniture, sidewalks), 1500-2000 PSI with 1.2-1.5 GPM is adequate. For more stubborn concrete, siding, or large decks, aim for 2000+ PSI and 1.5+ GPM. We recommend at least 1.2 GPM for effective rinsing.
  • Power Source: All options here are corded electric. Consider cord length and whether you have outdoor outlets. For ultimate portability without a cord, a gas model would be needed, but it will exceed the $200 budget.
  • Included Accessories: A good nozzle set (at least 3-5 tips: 0°, 15°, 25°, 40°, and soap) is crucial. A built-in soap tank or detergent injection system is a major plus for car washing.
  • Build Quality & Durability: Look for steel wands (vs. plastic), sturdy wheels, and a robust frame. Check reviews for mentions of leaks, motor burnout, and overall longevity.
  • Portability & Storage: If space is tight, look for a compact design, carry handle, and integrated wand storage.
  • Attachments vs. Full Units: First, decide if you need a full pressure washer or if an undercarriage cleaner attachment would benefit your existing setup. Attachments are useless without a compatible base unit.
