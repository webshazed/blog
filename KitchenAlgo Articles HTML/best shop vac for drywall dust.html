Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Drywall Dust Shop Vac

Drywall dust is exceptionally fine, abrasive, and electrostatically charged, making it prone to escaping filters and damaging motors. Here are the non-negotiable features for your next shop vac:

  • HEPA Filtration is Critical: Look for a unit that either includes a certified HEPA filter or explicitly states it is compatible with one. A "high-efficiency" or "fine dust" filter is not enough. HEPA (High-Efficiency Particulate Air) filters capture 99.97% of particles 0.3 microns in size—the exact size of drywall dust. This protects your health and prevents a fine white film from coating everything in the room.
  • Suction Power (Peak HP or CFM): You need enough power to lift dust from textured surfaces and corners. For residential projects, 3-5 Peak HP in a 5-6 gallon unit is a great sweet spot. Higher HP is better but often comes with more weight and noise.
  • Capacity: A 2.5-gallon tank will fill quickly with drywall dust. For anything more than a quick touch-up, a 5 or 6-gallon model is strongly recommended to minimize constant emptying.
  • Sealed System: The vacuum should have a sealed design with gaskets on the lid, filter housing, and collection ports to prevent dust leaks from joints.
  • Durability: Look for steel drums (not plastic) for long-term durability and resistance to impacts. Reinforced hoses and sturdy casters are also important for job site conditions.
  • Wet/Dry Capability: While you're using it for dry dust now, this feature adds future versatility for spills, flooding, or workshop cleanup.
  • Attachments: A crevice tool is essential for corners and edges. A brush attachment is useful for textured surfaces. A wide nozzle is good for floors. Consider if the attachments are robust and store easily on the unit.
