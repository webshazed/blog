Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Metal-Cutting Hole Saw Kit

When selecting a hole saw kit specifically for metal, prioritize these features:

  • Bi-Metal Construction: This is non-negotiable for metal. Look for "bi-metal" or "HSS" (high-speed steel) teeth welded to a flexible alloy body. Avoid carbon steel or standard HSS-only saws, as they will dull quickly on metal.
  • Size Range: Consider the metal thicknesses and hole diameters you commonly need. Kits with sizes from 3/4" to 2-1/2" cover most electrical and plumbing tasks. Larger diameters (up to 6") are needed for ductwork or custom fabrication.
  • Arbor Quality: A sturdy, precision arbor prevents wobble and ensures clean holes. Some kits use a universal arbor with shanks; others have dedicated arbors per size. Universal arbors are more convenient.
  • Chip Removal: Deep, wide slots between teeth are crucial for metal cuttings to eject chips and prevent binding/overheating.
  • Dust Management: For enclosed spaces (like cutting in ceilings or machinery), a dust bowl or collection attachment is a valuable safety and convenience feature.
  • Brand Reputation: Professional brands like Milwaukee, DeWalt, and Lenox invest in better alloys and tooth geometry, offering longer life and faster cuts—worth the premium for frequent use.
  • Warranty: Good brands stand behind their products. Look for at least a 1-year warranty, with some offering lifetime guarantees against breakage.

Remember: Always use cutting oil or lubricant when cutting thicker or harder metals (like steel plate or stainless). This extends blade life and improves cut quality.
