Best Juicer For Leafy Greens Reviewed In 2026

Juicing leafy greens like spinach, kale, and wheatgrass is a powerhouse way to boost your nutrient intake, but not all juicers are up to the task. Leafy greens are fibrous and require a slow, masticating action to extract maximum juice without clogging or overheating. In 2026, cold press and slow masticating juicers lead the pack for leafy greens, offering high juice yields, minimal oxidation, and quiet operation. This guide reviews the top 7 juicers tailored for leafy greens, helping you choose the perfect machine for daily wellness.

When selecting a juicer for leafy greens, prioritize models with slow RPMs (typically under 100 RPM), wide feed chutes to reduce prep time, and easy cleanup features. Centrifugal juicers, while fast, often struggle with leafy greens, producing less juice and more foam. Based on performance, user reviews, and features, we’ve curated the best options that excel with fibrous vegetables and herbs.

Buying Guide: How to Choose the Best Juicer for Leafy Greens

Selecting the right juicer for leafy greens involves key factors:

  • Speed (RPM): Opt for slow masticating juicers (40-100 RPM) to gently crush leaves, maximizing juice extraction and minimizing oxidation. Centrifugal models (10,000+ RPM) are too fast, causing greens to spin out without full juicing.
  • Feed Chute Size: A wider chute (5" or more) reduces prep time by allowing whole or large pieces of leafy greens to be fed without extensive chopping.
  • Juice Yield and Dryness of Pulp: High-quality masticating juicers produce drier pulp, indicating efficient extraction. Check user reviews for specific mentions of kale, spinach, or wheatgrass performance.
  • Ease of Cleaning: Look for dishwasher-safe parts and simple assembly. Leafy greens can be fibrous and sticky, so easy cleanup is essential for daily use.
  • Versatility: Some models make nut milk, sorbet, or pasta, adding value. However, prioritize leafy green performance over extra functions.
  • Noise Level: Slow juicers are generally quieter, making them better for household use, especially in the morning.
  • Durability and Warranty: A longer warranty (e.g., 10 years) indicates build quality, important for frequent juicing of tough greens.

Avoid centrifugal juicers if leafy greens are your primary produce; they often clog and yield less juice. Instead, invest in a cold press masticating juicer for consistent, nutrient-rich results.
