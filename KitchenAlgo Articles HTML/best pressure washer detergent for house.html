Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Detergent for Your House

Selecting the best detergent goes beyond just brand or rating. Consider these key factors for your specific project:

  • Surface Material: This is paramount. Vinyl and aluminum siding are generally robust. Wood requires gentler, pH-neutral cleaners to avoid stripping finishes or raising fibers. Stucco and brick can handle stronger formulas but are porous, requiring good rinsing. Always check the manufacturer's recommendations for your siding.
  • Primary Soiling Type:
    • General Dirt & Pollen: A versatile all-purpose cleaner like the Simpson or ZEP is perfect.
    • Organic Growth (Mildew/Algae/Lichen): Seek oxygen-based formulas (often labeled "Oxy Solve" or similar). Simple Green's Total Outdoor or House & Siding versions are top picks here.
    • Grease/Oil Stains: Near a road or have a grill exhaust? A heavy-duty degreaser like Simple Green Pro HD is necessary, but use with caution and proper dilution.
    • Set-In Mold Stains: The black streaks? Use a dedicated stain remover like RMR-86 as a pre-treatment before washing.
  • Concentration & Value: All products here are concentrates. Check the dilution ratio. A 1:10 ratio is common. A larger, more concentrated bottle offers better long-term value. Calculate the cost per cleaning session.
  • Environmental & Plant Safety: Biodegradable formulas are better for your garden and local waterways. Oxygen-based cleaners are typically more eco-friendly than chlorine or heavy solvents. Keep any detergent off desirable plants by wetting them first and rinsing after cleaning.
  • Compatibility: Ensure the detergent is safe for your pressure washer's pump (most modern concentrates are). Some "detergent" packages for big-box store washers are low-quality; professional-grade concentrates are always a better choice.
