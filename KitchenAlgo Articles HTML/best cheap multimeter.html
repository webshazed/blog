Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Cheap Multimeter

When shopping for an affordable multimeter, don't just look at the price. Focus on these key features to ensure you get a tool that is safe, accurate, and fit for purpose:

  • True RMS (TRMS): This is the single biggest factor for accuracy if you'll measure AC voltage (like from a wall outlet or an inverter). Standard meters are calibrated for perfect sine waves, but most real-world AC power is "noisy." A True RMS meter gives you correct readings regardless of waveform distortion. For serious AC work, prioritize TRMS.
  • Safety Category (CAT) Rating: This indicates the meter's ability to withstand voltage spikes and protect you. For home/auto use, CAT III 600V is the minimum safe standard. Avoid any meter without a clear CAT rating.
  • Counts & Resolution: The "count" (e.g., 2000, 4000, 6000) determines the display's resolution. Higher counts allow for more precise readings (e.g., 199.9V vs. 199V). 4000+ is excellent for the price.
  • Auto-Ranging vs. Manual: Auto-ranging automatically selects the correct range, making it far easier for beginners. Manual-ranging requires you to dial in the expected range, which can be educational but slower and riskier if you guess wrong.
  • Essential Functions: Ensure it measures AC/DC volts, resistance (ohms), continuity (with audible beep), and diode test. Additional useful features include Non-Contact Voltage (NCV), capacitance, frequency, and temperature.
  • Build & Display: Look for a rubberized case for drop protection and a bright, backlit LCD display for use in poor lighting.
