    Buying Guide: What to Look for in a Pool Pump Timer

    Choosing the right timer hinges on understanding your pool's needs and the timer's specs. Here are the critical factors:

    • Amperage (A) & Horsepower (HP) Rating: This is non-negotiable. Check your pool pump's motor nameplate for its Full Load Amps (FLA) or Rated HP. Your timer's rating must equal or exceed this value. Most small-to-medium residential pumps are 15A/1/2HP. Under-rating is a fire hazard.
    • Type: Mechanical vs. Digital
      • Mechanical (24-Hour): Simple, reliable, no batteries. Set once for the same daily schedule. Best for "set and forget" users.
      • Digital (7-Day): Programmable for different days. More flexible for seasonal changes. Requires a backup battery for power outages.
    • Weatherproofing & Certifications: Must be rated for outdoor use (look for "Outdoor" or "Weatherproof" in the name). Ensure it has a safety certification like ETL, UL, or CSA listing. The housing should be fully enclosed with a gasketed lid.
    • Outlets: Most have two grounded outlets. Ensure the cord length is sufficient to reach your pump and any other device (like a light).
    • Installation: Plug-in timers are easiest. Hardwired switches (like the Intermatic) offer a permanent solution but require an electrician or advanced DIY skills.
    • Intervals: Mechanical timers typically use 30-minute tabs. Digital timers can often set times down to the minute.
