Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right Pool Salt System

Selecting the best salt system requires evaluating your pool’s specific needs. Here are key factors to consider:

  • Pool Size and Type: Ensure the system or salt quantity matches your pool’s volume. Systems like the PoolRX+ are rated for specific gallon ranges (e.g., 7.5k–20k gallons). For salt, calculate your pool’s volume to determine how many bags you need—typically 1-2 lbs of salt per 100 gallons to reach the ideal 3000-4000 ppm.
  • System Compatibility: Not all salt products work with every chlorine generator. Pure, fine-grained salts (like FreshWater or Morton) are universally compatible. Avoid salts with anti-caking agents or iodine, which can damage cells. If you have a specific brand (e.g., FreshWater, Hayward), check manufacturer recommendations.
  • Salt Purity and Dissolution Rate: Look for 99%+ pure salt. Fast-dissolving salts (like FreshWater 80000) reduce wait times after startup, while coarser salts (like Morton) are economical but may take longer. For spas, use salts formulated for warm water to prevent scaling.
  • Maintenance and Accessories: Consider replacement cartridges (like FreshWater’s) for systems that use them, and keep salt-compatible shock (like HTH Salt Shock) on hand for superchlorination. A salt test kit is essential for monitoring levels.
  • Climate and Water Conditions: In areas with heavy rainfall or high evaporation, you’ll need to add salt more frequently. Hard water areas may benefit from scale-reducing additives or cartridges to protect the salt cell.
  • Budget: Balance upfront costs (system units) with long-term expenses (salt bags, shock, cartridges). A premium system like PoolRX+ may cost more initially but save on maintenance. Bulk salt purchases (e.g., Morton 40lb) offer better value for large pools.
