Buying Guide: Key Features to Consider Under $200

When shopping for a table saw in this budget range, focus on these critical features to ensure you get the best tool for your needs:

  • Motor Power: Most quality portable saws under $200 use 15-amp motors. Avoid anything below 13 amps for consistent performance in hardwoods. RPM (revolutions per minute) above 4,500 yields smoother cuts.
  • Blade Size: 10-inch blades are standard and can cut material up to 3-1/4 inches thick at 90°. 8-1/4 inch blades are common in compact saws, reducing depth capacity but increasing portability.
  • Rip Capacity: This is the maximum width of cut to the right of the blade. Look for at least 24 inches for versatility with plywood and lumber. Jobsite saws like the DEWALT DWE7491RS offer up to 32-1/2", which is exceptional for the price.
  • Portability: A folding or rolling stand is invaluable for moving the saw or saving space. Rolling stands are easier to move over long distances, while folding stands are lighter. Weight typically ranges from 45 to 90 pounds.
  • Fence System: A rack-and-pinion fence (found on DEWALT and SKIL models) provides smoother, more accurate adjustments than T-style fences. Ensure the fence is sturdy and doesn't flex during cuts.
  • Dust Collection: Even basic dust ports (1-1/4" or 2-1/2") allow connection to a shop vac. Better dust management keeps your workspace cleaner and improves visibility.
  • Safety Features: Non-negotiable elements include a blade guard, riving knife (or splitter), and anti-kickback pawls. Some models add electric brakes for faster blade stoppage.
