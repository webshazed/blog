Buying Guide: How to Choose the Best ATV Sprayer

  • Tank Capacity: For ATV use, larger tanks (4+ gallons) minimize refills. Backpack sprayers offer 4 gallons, while custom mounted systems can hold 10-20 gallons. Handhelds (1-2 gallons) are only for small jobs.
  • Power Source: Manual pumps are budget-friendly but tiring. Battery-powered backpacks reduce fatigue. For permanent ATV mounting, a 12V diaphragm pump (like our top pick) connected to the vehicle's electrical system is most efficient.
  • Nozzle Versatility: Look for adjustable nozzles or multiple nozzle tips. Cone nozzles cover wide areas; stream nozzles target weeds. For boom spraying, ensure nozzle compatibility.
  • Filtration System: Multi-stage filters prevent clogs from debris and chemical sediments—essential for fertilizers and wettable powders.
  • Durability & Materials: Chemical-resistant polyethylene tanks and brass/stainless steel components extend life. UV-resistant materials prevent sun damage during outdoor storage.
  • Mounting Compatibility: If building a custom system, check pump mounting brackets and hose lengths. Backpack sprayers are worn, not mounted, but some have straps that can be secured to an ATV seat.
  • Pressure Control: Adjustable pressure settings allow switching between delicate plants and tough weeds. Battery-powered models often have PSI gauges; manual pumps rely on pump strokes.
  • Weight Distribution: For backpacks, padded straps and waist belts improve comfort during long treks from your ATV to the treatment area.
