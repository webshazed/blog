Buying Guide: What to Look for in 2026

Choosing the right battery-powered weed eater isn’t just about brand reputation. Here are the critical factors to consider based on your specific needs:

1. Battery Voltage & Ecosystem

Voltage (V) generally correlates with power. For most residential use, 20V (or 18V) is sufficient. For heavier, overgrown areas, look at 40V, 56V (like EGO), or 80V platforms. Ecosystem compatibility is crucial. If you already own batteries and chargers from a brand like DEWALT, EGO, or CRAFTSMAN, buying a "tool-only" model saves significant money. Investing in a platform means all your batteries work across tools.

2. Cutting Swath (Width)

Measured in inches, this is how wide a path the trimmer cuts. For small yards (<1/4 acre), 10-12 inches is fine. For medium to large properties (1/4 - 1/2 acre), 14-16 inches will dramatically reduce mowing/trimming time. Wider isn’t always better if the unit becomes too heavy or unwieldy for your body type.

3. Weight & Ergonomics

You’ll be holding this tool at arm’s length. Check the weight (with battery) and note the shaft design. Straight shafts are better for tall users and undergrowth. Curved shafts are typically lighter and more intuitive for average-height users. Telescopic shafts (like on EGO models) are the ultimate adjustability feature.

4. Line Feed System

Bump Feed: You tap the head on the ground to release more line. Traditional, reliable, but you must stop to check/adjust line.
Auto Feed: Line automatically advances as you work (e.g., BLACK+DECKER). More convenient.
POWERLOAD/Rapid Reload: Push a button to feed line instantly (EGO). This is the gold standard for convenience, eliminating all guesswork and stops.

5. Runtime & Battery Capacity

Battery capacity is measured in Amp-hours (Ah). Higher Ah = longer runtime. A 2.0Ah battery might last 30-45 minutes on medium duty. A 4.0Ah or 5.0Ah battery can last 60-90 minutes. For large jobs, consider either a high-capacity battery or purchasing a second battery. Also, look for tools with brushless motors—they are more efficient, provide more power, and extend battery life.

6. 2-in-1 (Trimmer/Edger) vs. Dedicated

Many models offer an edger attachment (either a blade or a wheel-guided edger head). This is highly convenient for maintaining a clean lawn edge without a separate tool. However, dedicated edgers (especially blade-based ones) are often more robust and effective for heavy concrete edging. Assess your primary need.
