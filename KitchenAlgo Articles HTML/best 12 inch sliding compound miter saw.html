DEWALT DWS780

The DEWALT DWS780 stands as a benchmark for professional-grade sliding compound miter saws. The beastly 15-amp motor delivers power smoothly, and the dual-bevel capability (48° left/right) combined with a robust sliding mechanism handles massive stock with ease. Build quality is exceptional, with a rigid steel base and precise detents.

Pros Cons

  • Excellent dust collection port (works very well with a vacuum).
  • Extremely rigid and durable construction; holds calibration.
  • Smooth, accurate sliding action and positive miter/bevel stops.
  • Premium price point; a significant investment.
  • Heavy (over 50 lbs), less portable without a stand.

For professionals and demanding woodworkers who cannot compromise on accuracy, repeatability, or dust management, the DWS780 is arguably the best in its class. It’s a tool you’ll buy once and rely on for a decade. The included blade wrench and material clamp are nice touches.

Buying Guide: How to Choose the Right 12-Inch Sliding Compound Miter Saw

Beyond our top picks, here are the critical factors to match a saw to your specific needs:

  • Sliding vs. Non-Sliding: A sliding mechanism increases the maximum crosscut capacity significantly (often 12-16 inches). If you regularly cut wide trim, beams, or panels, a sliding saw is essential. For primarily smaller trim and molding, a non-sliding compound saw might suffice and is more compact.
  • Bevel Capacity: Most are dual-bevel (tilting left and right). This is crucial for complex crown molding and joinery without flipping the workpiece. Check the maximum bevel angle (45° is standard, some go to 48°+).
  • Motor Power & RPM: Look for a 15-amp motor for consistent power. Higher RPM (like 4,000) can yield cleaner cuts in wood but may increase blade wear. For hardwoods or dense materials, torque is more important than top speed.
  • Cutting Capacity & Fence: Note the stated crosscut capacity at 90° and 45°. A taller, rigid fence with adjustable stops adds versatility, especially for vertical cuts.
  • Dust Collection: This is not just about cleanliness; it's about visibility and health. A saw with a well-designed dust port that connects to a shop vac (2.5" or 4" port) will dramatically improve your experience. Look for models with enclosed chutes.
  • Cut-LineVisibility: Features like DEWALT's XPS, Bosch's laser, or Metabo's Xact Cut are game-changers for accuracy. If you do precise finish work, this is a worthy investment.
  • Portability vs. Weight: Sliding 12-inch saws are heavy (45-60+ lbs). If you need to move it often, consider weight, handle design, and potential for a rolling stand. Cordless models offer the ultimate portability but require battery investment.
  • Build Quality & Calibration: A rigid base and precise gearing are vital for cuts that stay true over time. Read reviews about calibration retention and overall "feel."
