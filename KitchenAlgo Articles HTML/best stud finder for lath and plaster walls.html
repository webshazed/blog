Buying Guide for Lath and Plaster Walls

When selecting a stud finder for lath and plaster, consider these key factors to ensure optimal performance:

  • Sensor Technology: Opt for multi-sensor or wide-scan models (like Franklin’s 9 or 13-sensor units) that can handle uneven surfaces by averaging readings across a larger area.
  • Live Wire Detection: Essential for safety; choose a stud finder with AC wire detection to avoid hazardous contacts during installations.
  • Material Compatibility: Ensure the device detects both wood and metal studs, as lath and plaster walls often contain metal lath or a mix of materials.
  • Display and Alerts: Look for clear LCD screens or LED indicators with audio alarms to confirm stud locations audibly, especially when scanning in awkward positions.
  • Magnetic vs. Electronic: Magnetic stud finders (like StudBuddy) are simple and effective on lath with metal fasteners but lack wire detection. Electronic models offer more versatility and safety features.
  • Calibration and Ease of Use: Choose tools with automatic or easy calibration for rough walls. User-friendly interfaces reduce frustration.
  • Durability and Power: For frequent use, consider build quality and battery type—rechargeable options or included batteries add convenience.
