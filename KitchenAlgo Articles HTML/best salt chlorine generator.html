Buying Guide for Salt Chlorine Generators and Accessories

When selecting products for a salt chlorine system, consider these key factors:

  • Pool or Spa Size: Generator cells are rated for specific volumes (e.g., up to 40,000 gallons). Choose a cell that matches or exceeds your water volume for efficient chlorine production.
  • Salt Compatibility: Use only salt labeled for chlorine generators. Avoid iodized or table salt, as additives can damage cells. Evaporated or solar salts are preferred for high purity.
  • Stabilizer Needs: Outdoor pools require cyanuric acid (stabilizer) to protect chlorine from UV breakdown. Test levels regularly; aim for 30-50 ppm.
  • Cell Maintenance: Look for cells with reverse polarity or self-cleaning features to reduce scale buildup. Hard water areas may need more frequent cleaning.
  • System Integration: Ensure all accessories (salts, cartridges) are compatible with your generator brand. Mixing incompatible products can void warranties.
  • Budget: Generators vary from mid-range to premium. Factor in long-term costs like salt and cell replacement (typically every 3-5 years).
  • Warranty and Support: Opt for brands with good customer service and warranties, especially for electronic components.

For salt selection, prioritize purity (99%+ sodium chloride) and granule size for quick dissolution. In hot tubs, use finer salts designed for smaller volumes to prevent excess foaming.
